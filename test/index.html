Vifi is a leading VOD service in Estonia. Operated since 2010, Vifi has over 10,000 movie and TV serie titles in its collection. In 2014, it launches an application for Samsung Smart TV - the first of its kind in the Baltic region. I am responsible for re-designing its web's interface as well as establishing a uniform recognation for the whole Vifi brand accross different platforms.

A five years old interface

Vifi's layout was very outdated, partly because they could not predict how fast can the service grow in just a couple of years. The structure of the website is very simple, almost too simple that it cannot host all the new features that have been added continuosly. As Vifi grew, the layout has become more and more inconsistent. Besides, the website is not responsive. It's just the matter of time until the company owners realized that they need a better user interface.

Not just better...

The first thing I knew that I should do with Vifi is, of course, to deliver its visitor a cleaner and more modern experience. It should be approachable enough for everybody, from a school girl to a retired grandma, to be able to use. Besides, since there are more people watching videos on their mobile devices, responsiveness is one of the key points to boost the service's accessibility. Last but not least, the layout should also be flexible and easy to update.

Search. Pay. Watch.

Because Vifi has a very extensive library, I understood that we have to focus on making the search function really simple so that users can find what they want quickly. In the new design, besides searching by keywords, users can also filter the result based on category, release date and length. I kept the filter bar really clear with a minimalistic look. Users can switch betweens tabs by clicking or swifting their fingers on a touch screen. As soon as they tick a check box, the result list will be shorterned down immediately. The whole experience feel effortlessly smooth.

A seamless experience across different devices

In 2014, Vifi partnered with Samsung to launch the first VOD application for smart TV in Estonia. They attempted to bring Vifi's service to more than 500,000 households with Samsung TV. The TV app should explore the uniqueness of smart TV's OS, and at the same time reflect Vifi's signature appearance. Design UI for TV is very challenging. The screen size requires a different approach to layout construction. User's behavior is also different. Instead of mouse or touch screen, users interact with the devices through remote and gesture. Therefore, I needed to re-think the whole concept for the UI with four main points:

Navigation

Instead of clicking, users navigates in smart TV through the hotkeys: up, down, left and right. Our navigation should be categorized according to this.

Readability

Although the screen size of the TV is bigger, but the actual effective area for content is much less. People don't want to read a lot of info on a TV screen.

Simplicification

We could not bring everything from the desktop version to the TV one, at least in the first release. Basic features must feel great to use.

Continuity

The TV app does not stand alone, nor do the desktop and mobile apps. The users want to switch between devices without any interuption.
